# Hoczi User Guide

A practical guide for **students** and **teachers** on how to use the Hoczi platform. This document explains how to sign up, navigate the interface, and use the core features designed for learning and teaching.

---

## 1. Welcome to Hoczi

Hoczi is a learning platform that helps teachers organize lessons and helps students study, practice, and track their progress. This guide walks you through everything you need to get started — no technical background required.

**Who this guide is for:**

- Students who want to learn at their own pace
- Teachers who want to manage classes, assignments, and student progress

---

## 2. Getting Started

### 2.1 Creating an Account

1. Open your web browser and go to **https://hoczi.com**
2. Click **Sign Up** in the top-right corner
3. Choose your role: **Student** or **Teacher**
4. Enter your name, email, and a secure password
5. Check your email and click the verification link

> Tip: Use the email address provided by your school if your school has registered with Hoczi.

### 2.2 Logging In

1. Click **Log In** at the top of the homepage
2. Enter your email and password
3. Click **Continue**

If you forget your password, click **Forgot password?** and follow the instructions sent to your email.

### 2.3 Setting Up Your Profile

After your first login, take a moment to:

- Add a profile photo
- Confirm your display name
- Set your language and time zone
- (Teachers) Add your subject areas and school

---

## 3. Quick Tour of the Interface

When you log in, you will see four main areas:

| Area | What it does |
|---|---|
| **Dashboard** | Your home page with recent activity and shortcuts |
| **Classes** | Where lessons, assignments, and class members live |
| **Library** | Learning materials, exercises, and saved resources |
| **Messages** | Notifications and direct messages with teachers/students |

A search bar at the top helps you find lessons, classmates, or topics quickly.

---

## 4. For Students

### 4.1 Joining a Class

There are two ways to join a class:

- **Class code** — your teacher will give you a short code (e.g., `ABC-123`). Click **Join Class** on the dashboard and enter it.
- **Invitation link** — click the link sent to you by email; you'll be added automatically.

### 4.2 Viewing Lessons

1. Open the **Classes** tab
2. Select your class
3. Click any lesson to open it

Each lesson may include:

- Reading material or video
- Interactive exercises
- A discussion area
- A quiz at the end

### 4.3 Submitting Assignments

1. Open an assignment from your class page
2. Read the instructions carefully
3. Type your answer or upload a file
4. Click **Submit**

You can review your work and grades after the teacher returns it. Late submissions are highlighted in orange.

### 4.4 Tracking Your Progress

The **Progress** section in your dashboard shows:

- Lessons completed
- Average score on quizzes
- Streak (consecutive days of activity)
- Areas where you may need more practice

Use this to plan your study sessions and focus on weaker topics.

### 4.5 Getting Help

- Ask your teacher through the **Messages** tab
- Use the **Discussion** area inside a lesson to ask classmates
- Click the **?** icon (bottom right) to view tutorials and FAQs

## 5. For Teachers

### 5.1 Creating a Class

1. Go to the **Classes** tab
2. Click **+ New Class**
3. Enter the class name, subject, and grade level
4. Click **Create**
5. Share the **class code** or invite link with your students

### 5.2 Adding Lessons

1. Open your class
2. Click **+ Add Lesson**
3. Choose a template or start from scratch
4. Add content blocks: text, video, image, file, or quiz
5. Click **Publish** when ready (or save as draft)

> Tip: You can reuse lessons across classes by clicking **Duplicate**.

### 5.3 Creating Assignments

1. Inside a class, click **+ Assignment**
2. Set the title, instructions, and due date
3. Choose the format: written response, file upload, or quiz
4. Set point value and grading rubric (optional)
5. Click **Assign**

Students will see the assignment immediately and receive a notification.

### 5.4 Grading and Feedback

1. Open the **Assignments** tab in your class
2. Click any submission to review
3. Add comments inline or in the feedback box
4. Enter a grade and click **Return**

You can grade in bulk by selecting multiple submissions and applying the same score or comment.

### 5.5 Tracking Class Performance

The **Insights** view shows:

- Average score per assignment
- Students who may need extra support
- Engagement (time spent, lessons completed)
- Quiz question difficulty analysis

Use these insights to adjust your teaching pace or provide targeted help.

### 5.6 Communicating with Students

- **Announcements** — post a message visible to the whole class
- **Messages** — send direct messages to individual students
- **Comments** — reply inside a lesson or assignment

## 6. Tips for Success

**For students:**

- Set a study schedule and stick to it
- Don't skip the quizzes — they help things stick
- Ask questions early; don't wait until the night before a deadline

**For teachers:**

- Mix lesson formats (video + reading + quiz) to keep students engaged
- Use the Insights view weekly to spot students who need help
- Celebrate progress — Hoczi lets you award badges and shout-outs

## 7. Common Questions

**I forgot my password — what now?**
Click **Forgot password?** on the login page and follow the email link.

**Can I use Hoczi on my phone?**
Yes. The website works on mobile browsers, and a mobile app is available for iOS and Android.

**My teacher hasn't graded my work yet.**
Grading times vary. Check the **Messages** tab if you have questions.

**Can students see each other's grades?**
No. Grades are private between the teacher and each student.

**How do I leave a class?**
Open the class settings and click **Leave Class**. You can rejoin with the class code anytime.

**I'm a teacher — can I export grades?**
Yes. In the **Assignments** tab, click **Export** to download grades as CSV or Excel.

---

## 8. Account and Privacy

- Your data is private and only visible to you, your teacher, or your students
- Update your password anytime from **Settings → Security**
- To delete your account, go to **Settings → Account → Delete account**

For more information, see the privacy policy linked at the bottom of every page.
